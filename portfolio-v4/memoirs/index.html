READ BEFORE YOU DIE

top 5 memoirs.

The Girl with Seven Names by Hyeonseo Lee | Changes your perspective on life

A gripping memoir that recounts the harrowing journey of a North Korean defector, Hyeonseo, escaping her oppressive homeland, adopting multiple identities, and ultimately finding freedom against all odds. This emotional odyssey serves as a deep look into a country rarely documented, making us realize the striking similarities we share as humans despite vastly different circumstances.

Maid by Stephanie Land | Deeply human

a compelling memoir that unveils the challenges faced by author Stephanie Land as a single mother working as a maid. Through poignant storytelling, Land shares the struggles of balancing low-paying jobs, navigating the welfare system, and striving for a better life for her daughter.

I'm Glad My Mom Died by Jeanette McCurdy | Cried 4 times reading this

explores the intricacies of childhood acting, delving into parental pressure, reflecting on mental health, and unraveling the complexities of friendships, particularly within the context of the toxic nature of "iCarly."

Born a Crime by Trevor Noah | Laugh, cry, an emotional rollercoaster

a captivating memoir that navigates his unconventional upbringing in apartheid-era South Africa, offering a poignant blend of humor, insight, and resilience.

Know My Name by Chanel Miller | I was angry the entire time

a powerful memoir that unveils the identity and resilience of the author, previously known as Emily Doe in the Brock Turner sexual assault case. Miller's narrative reflects on trauma, healing, and the pursuit of justice, offering a compelling perspective on the complexities of survivorship and the strength found in reclaiming one's name and story.
